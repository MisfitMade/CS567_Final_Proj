The Corsair Curse and Wilderness Chaos Altar


Dragon Slayer II is nearing completion but rather than make you wait until the 4th January, we've decided to release The Corsair Curse quest early! Alongside this, we bring forward the changes to the Chaos Altar as proposed in the Wilderness Rejuvenation II and your usual QoL and bug fixes.
​

The Corsair Curse


From the newly revealed town of Corsair Cove, deep in the south of Feldip Hills, the captain of the Corsairs has sailed to Port Sarim seeking help. His crew is sick, and he suspects that a curse has been laid upon them.

If you can unravel the fearful and conflicting tales of ogre relics, vengeful mermaids and demonic possession, maybe you can help his ailing crew overcome The Corsair Curse.


Captain Tock himself can be found at the crossroads north of Port Sarim, and has moored his ship next to the Rusty Anchor bar nearby. It’s a novice quest, requiring only that one defeat a level 35 mage and be unafraid of level 82 ogres.


Corsair Cove, and the quest, are completely accessible to non-members; Captain Tock will provide free transport for anyone who’s started his quest. For members, the charter ships provide an alternative route and there’s a short-cut from the southern reaches of the Feldip Hills as well as a Spirit tree teleport.

Along with the new quest, Corsair Cove features maple trees (which non-members can cut if they have completed the original Dragon Slayer), fishing spots and a new training area filled with level 82 ogres under the command of Chief Tess. You’ll have to help the Corsairs before they’ll let you use the bank, though.

​

The Chaos Altar


The Chaos Temple found within level 38 of the Wilderness, west of the Lava Maze, has received a lucrative buff for any adventurer brave enough to travel there.

Players can sacrifice bones on the Chaos Altar inside the temple with a 50% chance for the bone to not be consumed in the process of offering. The base XP gained is the same as a Gilded altar with 2 lit burners.


There is an NPC stood near the altar which will un-note any bones you hand them for a fee. The cost for this is 50gp per bone. This is the only benefit the NPC offers.

Make sure you meet the requirements for Dragon Slayer II in preparation for the release on the 4th January in order to get your very own cape!

Give as You Pay


As of Monday 4th December, we started trialling a new initiative that allows you to round up payments to the next whole value, should you wish to – with the round up excess going to our partner charities. All those pennies add up!

We’ll be testing this out for a few weeks to get your feedback before making any widespread, permanent changes. This means that, for the meantime, only some of you will have the option to give using this method. For those who do, it will be completely opt-in and transparent: if you don’t tick the box, no extra funds will be donated, and how much you’re giving will be made clear in the basket summary.

In Other News


Our Christmas event is nearly ready to be unwrapped. You can expect the update to go live on Monday 11th December, which also marks our last scheduled update of the year!

  • As of the next Deadman season, players will now have their grace period after a kill removed if they teleport. This is because players were abusing it to make it safely through dangerous areas. This change has been applied to the permanent Deadman world (345), effective immediately.
  • A deposit all button has been added to the raids private storage chest whilst inside a raid dungeon.
  • The "DMM Ref" items that could be seen in the Grand Exchange have been removed.
  • Private storage chests within raids have had their drag sensitivity tweaked, making it less likely to drag an item when attempting to withdraw it.
  • Invisible explosions will no longer damage the player during the Great Olm encounter when a bomb is no longer present.
  • Bursting crystals will no longer spawn when stood on top of a bomb during the Great Olm encounter.
  • Acid pools will no longer deal damage to the player if the pool is no longer present during the Great Olm encounter.
  • Falling crystals now cast a shadow when falling during the Great Olm encounter.
  • Fires can no longer be lit in the Great Olm boss room. They can however be lit in the lobby area.
  • Fixed some punctuation on the NPCs at Mount Quidamortem.
  • The Explorer's rings will now display how many charges remain after using them.
  • Imbued rings have had their value increased slightly to protected over unimbued versions.
  • The warning displayed for starting a raid close to the regular Thursday system updated can now be suppressed at the time of receiving it.
  • The new Mage Arena capes now work with the Charge spell in PvP.
  • Recoil damage once again works on Vasa Nistirio when it's in a combat form.
